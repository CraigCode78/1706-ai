07 · The evidence

Effort first. AI second.

MIT found that leaning on AI to do the thinking weakens memory and ownership. Doing the work first, then using AI to sharpen it, protects learning. The same tool, the opposite effect.

08 · Not theory

A country already chose to lead

64 to 90%

of Estonian pupils were already using AI before the national programme began.

  • So they trained teachers first, then gave pupils a guided tool that coaches rather than answers.
  • Their rule: not who uses AI the most, but who uses it the smartest.

Leading beats banning. The full case study sits behind this.

09 · What you may be facing

The tensions to name

  • Pupils already use AI, whatever the policy says.
  • Stopping misuse versus using AI to help learning.
  • Unaided exams, in a world that rewards AI fluency.
  • Staff confidence runs from keen to frightened.
  • Parents want younger children off screens, and older ones ready for an AI world.
  • Too many tools, and no obvious place to start.
