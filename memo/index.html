The work in front of us, and the two years that decide it.

A vision document for the team. Why this matters, why now, what we are building, and how we will get it done.

Craig Hepburn · Founding Partner For internal circulation

Most of what is written about artificial intelligence misses the human at the centre of it. People are anxious. Their attention is being eaten by tools that promised to free them. Their jobs feel under threat from a technology they did not ask for. They are being told the future will be different and they are not being told what to do about it on Monday morning. This is the ground we are standing on, and it is the same ground every leader, every team, every person who will buy from us is standing on too. If we cannot start there, we cannot start anywhere.

The shift we believe in is not from human to machine. It is from human attention being spent on the wrong things to human attention being spent on what only humans can do. Agents are the mechanism. Refocused attention is the outcome. Everything 1706 builds and sells points at that single idea.

The market has moved past tools. It has moved past pilots. It has moved past the question of whether agents work. The largest, most public companies in technology have made structural commitments in a single six month window, and the data behind them tells the same story.

The operator signal is louder than the analyst signal. Coinbase has cut fourteen per cent of its workforce to rebuild as an intelligence with humans around the edge. Every story about the big companies is free marketing for what we sell to the small ones. They are normalising the playbook in public. We are productising it.

§ 02 · The position

What people are actually buying, once you cut through the noise.

People do not want more AI. They want fewer tools, less noise, and their attention back. They want agents that take the operational grunt work away. They want their teams to stop fearing for their jobs and start running faster than they ever have. They want to look at their week and see space for the work that matters, instead of the work that fills the calendar.

Every conversation we have had this season points at the same thing. Vecto. The portfolio companies. The founders and operators reaching out unprompted. The number one ask is not strategy and it is not software. It is agents that operate inside our business. Demand is already coming through the door. The job is to ship.

Where we sit, deliberately.

We are not a consultancy. Consultancies sell time. We are not a software vendor. Vendors sell licences. We are not a no-code agent platform. Platforms sell tools. We are a productised agent factory. We codify how a business operates and ship the agents that run it. The catalogue grows. The methodology compounds. The clients accumulate. The business scales.

§ 03 · The model

The five layers of the AI operating model.

The factory has a product. The product is the AI Operating Model: a complete, structured way of redesigning a business so that agents can operate inside it and humans can do the work only they can do. Five layers, in order. Each one earns the layer above.

  1. Clarity Layer
    What genuinely matters in this business right now. Where leadership attention should go. The North Star against which everything else is measured.
  2. Operating Context Layer
    The structured codification of how the business actually operates. Positioning, ICP, processes, decisions, voice, all written for humans and agents to read together. The connective tissue. The thing that turns scattered knowledge into operational fuel.
  3. Agent Layer
    Operational agents from a defined catalogue, deployed against the context layer. We start with one, prove it, expand. Each agent is version controlled, scoped, and accountable.
  4. Orchestration and Governance
    Identity, action history, confidence thresholds, blast radius, cost. The leader can answer at any moment what the agents did, when, and at what cost.
  5. Human Operating Layer
    Roles redesigned, teams reshaped, attention refocused. AI fluency as a baseline. Humans above the loop, in the loop where it matters, never under it.
§ 04 · The opportunity, in numbers

A small slice is enough.

The temptation is to inflate the maths. The more honest move is to show how forgiving the maths actually is. The agent market alone is heading from roughly eight billion dollars in 2025 to over fifty billion by 2030. The UK has approximately five and a half million SMEs. The EU has twenty four million. The US has thirty three million. The segment that genuinely fits our offer (small and medium businesses with operational pain to solve, ten or more employees, willing to invest in transformation) is in the millions.

§ 05 · The two-year build

A company designed to scale, designed to exit.

Be honest about the shape of the bet. We are building a company that we intend to sell, or to take serious investment into, within roughly twenty four months. This is not a lifestyle business. It is not a long-form consultancy. It is a fast, focused, productised build aimed at a strategic acquisition or a significant institutional round at the point where the catalogue, the methodology, and the customer base are visibly compounding.

What gets acquired, at premium multiples.

Revenue alone is not what gets bought. Defensibility is. Three things compound inside 1706, and the team should hold them in mind in every decision they make.

Catalogue
Every agent we ship teaches us how to ship the next one cheaper. By engagement fifty, the catalogue is an asset a competitor cannot replicate without our history. This is the product moat.
Methodology
The way we codify a business into the operating context layer is genuinely novel. If we document it ruthlessly, refine it through repetition, and publish parts of it, it becomes the standard the industry references. Standards are durable moats.
Data
Every engagement produces evaluation data, configuration patterns, telemetry on which agents perform in which contexts. By engagement one hundred we know things no one else knows. This is the moat acquirers pay a premium for.

The plausible exit profile, two to three years out, is a strategic acquisition by a player who needs the methodology and the customer base to accelerate their own agent strategy. Salesforce. ServiceNow. Microsoft. A Big Four firm building out their AI services arm. Private equity rolling up the category. The multiples on high-growth, defensible AI services in this period are running between five and fifteen times revenue, depending on growth rate and margin.

No handoffs. No translation gaps. The strategy ships because the people who designed it are the people deploying it. Skin in the game
Equity that means something at exit, structured cleanly, communicated openly. The team builds the value, the team participates in the value. This is non-negotiable for the people we want.
A house, not a desk
1706 is a studio in the editorial sense. The work is intellectually serious, the writing is published, the standards are visible, the people in the room are the kind of people they wanted to be in the room with. Culture is a recruitment moat.

How we operate, internally.

We run the operating model on ourselves before we sell it. Our own context layer, our own agents, our own catalogue under construction. Every internal process becomes a candidate for codification. Every meeting transcribed into the context layer. Every decision logged. Every agent we build for ourselves becomes a product line for the catalogue. This is not a cute internal habit. It is the cheapest, fastest research and development we will ever do, and the credibility it gives us in client conversations is unfair.
