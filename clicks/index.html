i. What you get

When you engage me as an advisor, you get four things, and they travel together.

01 · Time
My time
A genuine commitment, not a notional one. One day per week as the baseline cadence, structured around what is actually useful in the moment. Working sessions with the founders, deeper dives where the questions warrant it, and asynchronous input when something needs a fast read.
02 · Thinking
My thinking
Two decades of operating context across telecoms, consumer hardware, and culture, sharpened by the last several years at the front edge of AI and agentic systems. Hardware companies need someone who has lived inside hardware and can hold a credible point of view on how AI changes the product, the company, and the operating model in parallel.
03 · Network
My network
A small team and wider network of AI and agentic engineers I work with regularly. They are not on standby for Clicks, but they are reachable when a conversation requires their depth, and they can be brought in to scope and deliver specific projects when we agree there is something worth building.
04 · Knowledge
My access to that knowledge
The advisor relationship gives Clicks a window into what is happening across the agentic landscape, what is working, what is not, and where the leverage is. That intelligence flows back into your operating decisions and your product roadmap continuously, not just in scheduled sessions.
ii. Why this is different from standard advisory

Most advisory relationships are about counsel. This one includes counsel, but the harder edge of what I bring is operational. Specifically, identifying where AI and agentic infrastructure can replace, augment, or pre empt cost inside Clicks. That means existing functions running leaner, and future hires you do not need to make because the work is being done by agents you already own.

I am doing this with a couple of other companies right now, and the pattern is consistent. A single well designed agent can absorb the work of a senior hire, often at a fraction of the annual cost.

Fig. 01 · Agent topology
A function reshaped, not removed The retainer should be read against what it removes from your cost base, not just what it adds to it.

That is the framing I work in.

iii. How we work together

The relationship has two distinct tracks. Keeping them separate is what makes the engagement clean and durable.

Track A · Continuous
The advisory track
This is the ongoing relationship. Strategic input across two areas. First, AI operations and agentic infrastructure inside Clicks, the operating model conversation we have been circling. Second, product and concept input on the hardware roadmap, particularly the communication device direction and how the product reflects the agentic thesis. Continuous, not transactional.
Track B · Discrete
The project track
Where a specific piece of build work emerges from the advisory conversations, something that needs the team rather than just my time, we scope it as a discrete project with its own brief, timeline, and fixed price. You see the cost before committing, you control which projects happen and when. If no projects are right, no projects happen. The advisory relationship continues either way.
Fig. 02 · Two tracks across the term
Continuous advisory, discrete projects The retainer buys you me. Projects are how the team gets engaged, when and if you want them.
iv. The shape of the engagement
Term
24 months With a six month review point for both sides. Thirty day exit notice on either side after the review.
Retainer
£10,000 per month Payable monthly in advance. If that feels heavy for where Clicks is right now, let me know and we can talk it through, I can explain the thinking behind it.
Equity
Modest allocation, milestone vesting Standard advisor vesting with a three month cliff, then milestone based vesting over the term as discussed with Kevin. Open to landing inside the range Clicks works in with other advisors, assuming it sits in a sensible band for the value being exchanged.
Exclusivity
Narrow and specific I will not advise companies building directly competing hardware products in the smartphone accessory or dedicated AI communication device categories. I retain the right to advise adjacent companies and continue my own commercial work outside the engagement.
Confidentiality
Standard mutual NDA Anything sensitive shared in either direction stays inside the relationship.
