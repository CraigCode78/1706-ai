§ 01 · The shift

Three quarters of businesses use AI. One per cent get value from it.

The technology works. The models are capable. The agents are real. The thing missing in nine out of ten companies is the structure that lets agents operate against the actual context of the business.

People are buried under tools that promised to free them. Their attention is the scarcest resource in the company, and almost every AI deployment is making it worse, not better. The shift is not from human to machine. It is from attention spent on the wrong things to attention spent on what only humans can do.

85%
struggle to find AI ROI
Cause cited consistently across McKinsey, Lopez Research and Deloitte: fragmented data, missing operating context.
46%
CAGR for the agent market
Three independent forecasts converge on roughly seven times growth between 2025 and 2030. The gap between adoption and value is the largest opportunity in enterprise software since the cloud.

§ 04 · The model

Five layers, in order. Each one earns the layer above.

The factory has a product. The product is a complete way of redesigning a business so agents can operate inside it and humans can do the work only they can do.

Clarity
Where leadership attention should go. The North Star against which everything else is measured.
Operating context
The structured codification of how the business actually runs. The connective tissue. The thing that turns scattered knowledge into operational fuel.
Agents
Operational agents from a defined catalogue, deployed against the context layer. We start with one. We prove it. We expand.
Orchestration
Identity, action history, confidence thresholds, blast radius, cost. The leader can answer at any moment what happened, when, and at what cost.
Human operating
Roles redesigned, teams reshaped, attention refocused. AI fluency as a baseline. Humans above the loop, in the loop where it matters, never under it.
